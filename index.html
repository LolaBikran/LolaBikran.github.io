My story
From Struggle to Transformation

Like many of you, I’ve felt anxious, lost, and overwhelmed by the world. I didn’t always see things as I do now. In fact, for much of my life, I believed something was wrong with me. I thought my struggles with identity, mental pain, and feeling disconnected were flaws that needed fixing.

  • Childhood:
    Instability and Loss

    Growing up, I was surrounded by instability and emotional challenges. Losing my parents at a young age, I was raised by family members who loved me but also carried their own heavy burdens of trauma. This left me struggling with self-esteem, emotional outbursts, and an overwhelming need for approval.

  • Adolescence & Early twenties:
    Grappling with Identity

    In my early twenties, I found myself living a carefree and escapist lifestyle in Paris. I focused on socializing, partying, drinking, and smoking. While these years brought many fun memories, I couldn’t shake the feeling that something was out of sync. For a long time, I believed my sensitivity and emotional intensity were weaknesses that I had to hide or overcome. I felt like I didn’t fit into the mold society expected.

  • Pursuing Health Studies
    Delusion

    Even as I pursued my passion for Life Sciences and health, something felt off. I earned a master’s degree in nutrition and spent years studying how the human body works, but the conventional approach to healthcare felt reductive. It focused too much on diagnosis and treatment, overlooking the deep connection between the mind, body, and environment. I began to realize that health isn’t just about physical well-being—it’s about how we engage with ourselves, others, and the world.

  • Turning Point:
    Starting questioning the societal narrative

    In my late twenties, after years of denying my sensitivity and trying to conform to societal expectations, I hit a turning point. I was in distress, overwhelmed by the very system that promised health and happiness but left me feeling stuck. That’s when I started to investigate what true well-being means—not just from a physical or mental standpoint, but holistically. I started asking bigger questions about the world, our environment, and how the pressures we face impact us as human beings.

  • The Last Two Years:
    Research and Transformation

    Through my own struggles with identity and mental health, I’ve come to understand that what we perceive as emotional ‘weakness’ is often an incredible tool for insight and growth. I realized that the anxiety I felt, the confusion and sense of being lost, weren’t signs of personal failure. They were signals—indications that something deeper was out of sync, not just in my life but in society as a whole.

    In addition to my personal experience, I have extensively studied psychology and depth psychology, exploring topics such as the sense of meaning, existentialism, and the fundamental human need for connection. These fields have illuminated how our search for meaning, our drive to form authentic social bonds, and our existential questions shape the way we live, heal, and transform. My sensitivity has given me the ability to not only navigate these questions personally but to deeply understand the experiences of others.

  • Present Day:
    Sharing the Journey

    Now, I’m passionate about sharing what I’ve learned with others. I believe that we’re at a pivotal moment in history, where the challenges we face are also opportunities for transformation. Through my work, I want to help people see that our anxiety, our sense of being lost, is not a problem to be fixed but a doorway to deeper awareness and growth. Together, we can reclaim our well-being and create a more compassionate, ethical, and connected world.

My vision

For collective healing and transformation

In today’s fast-paced, overwhelming world, many of us feel anxious, lost, and disconnected. We are bombarded with distractions, inundated with misinformation, and trapped in a system that tells us we’re the problem. The pressure to keep up, to push through anxiety, and to numb our struggles with quick fixes like medication creates a cycle where we feel increasingly disconnected from ourselves, from others, and from the planet.

But what if our anxiety, our sense of being utterly lost, isn’t a flaw? What if these feelings are natural responses to a world in collective denial?

We’ve become so focused on individual survival that we’re missing the bigger picture—the societal and environmental shifts happening all around us. The rise in mental health issues, public health crises, and societal unrest are symptoms of this larger collective issue. Our survival mode has kicked in, but we’re not meant to live in constant fight-or-flight.

Our system is not set up to nurture our well-being. Instead, it treats our anxiety and confusion as individual faults to be fixed rather than as signs of deeper, systemic problems. This creates a disconnect between the life we live and the life we could live. Many of us are waking up to this realization, but the path forward can seem daunting.



Here’s where zooming out becomes essential.



We need to step back from the day-to-day noise and look at the broader picture. We are at a unique moment in history, facing not just individual challenges but global ones. The world we once knew, where purpose was shaped by things like rebuilding after wars or serving our countries, has shifted. We are now faced with global, interconnected problems: environmental crises, the fragmentation of social bonds, and an economic system that no longer resonates with our values.



Yet, this moment of confusion is also our greatest opportunity for transformation.



When we zoom out, we see that the world is shifting toward a more holistic, global consciousness. We are no longer isolated in our struggles; we are all deeply connected. This awareness opens up a chance to reclaim our well-being and redefine how we live and interact with each other and the planet. It’s time to stop seeing our feelings of anxiety and overwhelm as personal failures and start seeing them as signals for change.



My vision is to help people realize that transformation is not just possible—it’s necessary. By understanding the link between the collective denial we’re experiencing and the public health issues that are rising, we can begin to heal, both individually and as a society. We must acknowledge that our current system isn’t built to support holistic well-being and that it's time to build a new, more ethical way of living—one that honors our humanity and the natural world.



I believe that by reclaiming our well-being and re-establishing authentic social bonds, we can create a future rooted in compassion, self-awareness, and ethical action. This isn’t about fixing ourselves or finding quick solutions to our struggles. It’s about opening a new door to a life that aligns with who we truly are and what the world truly needs.



We are in a unique position to use technology as a tool for reconnection, to reimagine our relationship with the earth, and to create communities that support one another in meaningful ways. Together, we can break free from the survival mode that has kept us stuck, and begin to live in a way that nurtures both individual and collective well-being.



Let’s step into this new world, where transformation is not just a dream—it’s our path forward.
